The Problem

Why global projects fail in Korea.

Korea is not a "translate and launch" market. It is a structurally different ecosystem with its own platforms, gatekeepers, cultural codes, and regulatory walls.

Platform Lock-Out

Korean users live on Naver, KakaoTalk, DC Inside, and Korean CT — not Google, Telegram, or Discord. Western marketing playbooks have zero reach.

Exchange Gatekeeping

Upbit controls 78% of volume. Without relationships and compliance infrastructure, listing is impossible. Bithumb is the only realistic alternative.

Cultural Mistranslation

Korean investors evaluate projects through institutional trust, not decentralization narratives. Messaging that works in CT doesn't convert in Korea.

KOL Fraud

60%+ of Korean crypto "influencers" listed on broker platforms use fabricated engagement. Without direct relationships, you pay for bots.

Regulatory Complexity

VASP registration, Travel Rule compliance, PIPA (privacy), crypto tax framework — the legal surface area is vast and enforcement is real.

Community Fragmentation

Korean communities are on KakaoTalk (closed) not Telegram (open). Building and moderating across fragmented platforms requires native operators.
